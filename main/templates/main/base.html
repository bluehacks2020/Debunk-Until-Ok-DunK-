Music of Indigenous People

Kutiyapi

- Two stringed lute made of wood, one string for the melody, one for the drone. Eight frets originally held in place placed on the neck of the lute by a sticky rubbery substance, propolis, produced by honey bees to repair damages and openings in the hive. The lute is decorated with floral motives; the tail is carved to represent a stylised crocodile head. Reference: https://www.kipas.nl/Instruments/Kudyapi.htm Price: Php 61,068.40 (Antique)

Kulintang

- Kulintang music is Filipino - the melodies of kulintang are based on traditional chanting, vocal music and boat-lute music that were eventually translated to the Kulintang ensemble. This used to be how our ancestors communicated with each other. They are actual voices, and the patterns on a piece are conjugations of each other, as if speaking a language. - Kulintang music is not part of any religious practice - Its association with Islam has become a tactic for the Spanish conquistadors to create a divide as they found it hard to infiltrate the Mindanao region. Calling kulintang Islamic music is like calling Philippine music Spanish. Reference: https://usa.inquirer.net/8190/7-basic-things-know-kulintang-music Price: Php 18,500

Gambang

A gambang, properly called a gambang kayu ('wooden gambang') is a xylophone-like instrument used among peoples of Indonesia and the southern Philippines in gamelan and kulintang, with wooden bars as opposed to the metallic ones of the more typical metallophones in a gamelan. A largely obsolete instrument, the gambang gangsa, is a similar instrument made with metal bars.
